I'm Andrew Gentry, and my spiritual journey has shaped everything I am today—on and off the field. After high school, I felt strongly about serving a mission for The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints before playing college football. I was originally called to Argentina, but due to the pandemic, I served in the Utah Orem Mission, Spanish-speaking. It was one of the most life-changing experiences of my life. Teaching the gospel and serving others deepened my faith and gave me clarity about what really matters.

While on my mission, Coach Mendenhall resigned from UVA, and that opened the door to meet with Coach Harbaugh from Michigan. With my mission president's permission, we had a quick meeting—just 15 minutes—and I committed to Michigan without even visiting. I trusted the Lord was guiding me.

After my mission, I played for Michigan, was part of a national championship team, and served in a YSA bishopric in Ann Arbor. Later, I transferred to BYU to be closer to family and continue growing spiritually and athletically. My mission taught me how to rely on the Lord daily—and that's something I carry with me every step of the way.
